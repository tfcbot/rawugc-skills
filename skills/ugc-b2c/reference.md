# B2C UGC Formats — Shot List Reference

Shot-by-shot breakdowns for all 15 B2C UGC formats with example products filled in. For prompt templates with `[BRACKETED]` placeholders, see [SKILL.md](SKILL.md). For full completed prompts, see [examples.md](examples.md).

**Defaults**: `aspectRatio: "portrait"`, `nFrames: "10"` unless noted.

---

## 1. Just Got This

**Vibe**: Immediate reaction to receiving or buying something — raw excitement, no prep.

**Example Product**: Stanley Quencher H2.0 Tumbler

- 0-3s: Holding the Stanley Quencher in Cream fresh out of the box, barely contained excitement, turning it over in hands
- 3-6s: Examining the build — running a finger along the handle, testing the straw mechanism (push-click), checking the rubber base grip
- 6-8s: Fill it with ice and water right there on the kitchen counter — first sip through the straw, eyebrows raise — "the flow rate is actually perfect"
- 8-10s: Looking at camera, definitive nod — "okay I get the hype now"; sets it down next to their old water bottle, which suddenly looks inadequate

**Settings**: `aspectRatio: "portrait"`, `nFrames: "10"`

## 2. A Day With This

**Vibe**: The product follows you through a normal day — it's a background character in your life.

**Example Product**: AirPods Pro

- 0-3s: Morning — popping AirPods in while scrambling eggs, a podcast playing, the spatial audio adjusting as they turn their head toward the stove
- 3-6s: Midday — sitting in a busy coffee shop, tapping the stem to switch to Active Noise Cancellation, the ambient chaos melting away, focus mode
- 6-9s: Evening — walking the dog at sunset, Transparency Mode on, hearing the neighborhood sounds mixed with music, one earbud out for safety
- 9-12s: End of day — AirPods case sitting on the nightstand, the small LED light glowing as it charges; they've been everywhere today without being noticed

**Settings**: `aspectRatio: "portrait"`, `nFrames: "15"`

## 3. The Comparison

**Vibe**: Your product vs the obvious alternative — honest, no-script take.

**Example Product**: Yeti Rambler vs Hydro Flask

- 0-3s: Both bottles on a clean counter — Yeti Rambler 26oz on the left, Hydro Flask 24oz on the right, same-ish color, both full of ice water
- 3-6s: Picking up each one — comparing the powder coat finish (Yeti's is grippier), the lid mechanism (Yeti's chug cap vs Hydro's flex cap), the weight in hand
- 6-8s: Eight hours later — same shot, checking both; the Yeti still has ice clinking, the Hydro has mostly melted; tipping both to demonstrate
- 8-10s: Setting the Yeti forward — "two extra hours of ice and the lid doesn't leak in my bag"; the comparison was fair, the result is honest

**Settings**: `aspectRatio: "portrait"`, `nFrames: "10"`

## 4. Hack I Found

**Vibe**: Unexpected way to use the product — "wait, try this."

**Example Product**: Command Strips

- 0-3s: Holding a pack of Command Strips with a mischievous "I figured something out" expression, standing at their messy desk
- 3-6s: Demonstrating the hack — peeling a strip, sticking a power strip to the underside of the desk, pressing firmly; then routing all the dangling cables up and securing them along the desk leg
- 6-8s: Camera shows the result: the desk went from a cable nightmare to completely clean — no visible wires, everything hidden underneath
- 8-10s: Sits down at the now-clean desk, puts feet up, arms behind head — "you're welcome" energy; a $7 hack that changed the whole workspace

**Settings**: `aspectRatio: "portrait"`, `nFrames: "10"`

## 5. Honest Pros and Cons

**Vibe**: Talking to camera, listing what you love and what's mid — full transparency.

**Example Product**: Kindle Paperwhite

- 0-3s: Holding the Kindle Paperwhite, talking to camera — "okay here's my actual honest take after six months"
- 3-6s: Pros — counting on fingers: "the screen is incredible in direct sunlight," "it's waterproof, I read in the bath," "battery lasts weeks not hours," "my eyes don't burn at midnight like with my phone"
- 6-8s: Cons — "page turns are a tiny bit laggy," "the cover is $40 extra which feels steep," "you can't easily share highlights with non-Kindle friends"
- 8-10s: Verdict — holding it up — "the pros demolish the cons, I haven't touched a physical book in months and my screen time is down 40 minutes a day"

**Settings**: `aspectRatio: "portrait"`, `nFrames: "10"`

## 6. Setup Timelapse

**Vibe**: Unbox to fully set up in a satisfying sped-up sequence.

**Example Product**: IKEA KALLAX Shelf Unit

- 0-3s: The flat-pack IKEA box on the living room floor, box cutter slicing the tape, pulling out the panels, hardware bag, and the instruction sheet
- 3-7s: Assembly sped up 3x — laying out all panels by size, hammering in wooden dowels, connecting the first two cubes, each screw going in with a satisfying spin
- 7-10s: The shelf stands upright — video slows to normal speed; sliding storage bins into the cubes, stacking books, placing a plant on top
- 10-15s: Step back to admire — the finished KALLAX against the wall, fully styled with books, bins, and a framed photo; the flat-pack box is gone, the room is transformed

## 7. The Sound It Makes

**Vibe**: ASMR focus on satisfying product sounds — clicks, zips, pours, snaps.

**Example Product**: HHKB Mechanical Keyboard

- 0-3s: Close-up of the HHKB sitting on a wood desk, fingers hovering over the Topre switches — anticipation
- 3-6s: First keypress — a deep, cushioned "thock" — then a slow deliberate typing sequence, each key producing that distinctive hollow sound
- 6-8s: Same keys from a different angle — side profile showing the key travel; rapid typing now, the thocks blending into a rhythmic cascade
- 8-10s: Final slow single press on the spacebar — the deepest thock — fingers rest on the keys; the sound is the entire story

**Settings**: `aspectRatio: "portrait"`, `nFrames: "10"`

## 14. POV You Own This

**Vibe**: First-person POV of daily life with the product — you're living it.

**Example Product**: Aeropress Coffee Maker

- 0-3s: POV — hands reach across the kitchen counter and pick up the Aeropress from its spot next to the kettle, the morning light hitting the clear chamber
- 3-6s: Scooping coffee grounds in, placing it on a mug, pouring hot water — the camera is the person's eyes watching the water darken through the chamber
- 6-8s: Pressing down — the slow, satisfying resistance of the plunge, the hiss at the bottom; lifting the Aeropress off, a concentrated shot of coffee in the mug below
- 8-10s: Adding hot water for an Americano, steam rising — picking up the mug and walking toward the window; the morning ritual took 90 seconds
