# UGC Content Grader — Full Reference

## Quality Score Rubric (1–10)

### 10 — Perfect
- Hook is instantly relatable and specific to the persona
- Overlay is tight (3–4 lines max), adds value without repeating the hook
- Shot list is simple, filmable, emotionally clear
- Angle (Pain, Fear, Outcome, etc.) lands naturally — not forced

### 9 — Excellent
- Strong hook that stops the scroll
- Overlay complements without over-explaining
- Shot list is clean, low complexity
- Minor nitpick at most (e.g. one line of overlay could be tighter)

### 7–8 — Good
- Hook works but could be sharper or more specific
- Overlay is slightly long or repetitive
- Shot list is fine but could have more personality
- Angle is present but not perfectly tuned to persona

### 5–6 — Mediocre
- Hook is generic or doesn't stop the scroll
- Overlay tries too hard or says too much
- Shot list has unnecessary complexity
- Angle feels forced or mismatched with persona

### 3–4 — Weak
- Hook sounds like everyone else's content
- Overlay is a wall of text or pure sales copy
- Shot list is unfocused or too ambitious for AI gen
- Persona mismatch — wrong language for the audience

### 1–2 — Broken
- Hook makes no sense or has no emotional pull
- Content is incoherent or completely off-brand
- Would not work on any platform

## Complexity Score Rubric

### Low (target)
- One static location (bedroom, bathroom, kitchen, couch, coffee shop)
- One person talking to camera
- Simple hand gestures (hold up fingers, shrug, point)
- No props that require precision placement
- No on-screen text that the AI needs to render
- No walking, no location changes mid-video

### Medium (rewrite recommended)
- ONE of: location change, precise prop interaction, brief readable text
- Example: "walk from kitchen to living room" — change to static at couch
- Example: "hold up phone showing analytics" — change to verbal description with finger count

### High (must rewrite)
- Screen content (phone screens, laptop displays, analytics dashboards)
- Two-phone setups
- Walking while talking
- Specific readable numbers or text the AI must render
- Multiple simultaneous props requiring coordination
- Before/after split screens

### Common Complexity Fixes
| Problem | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| "Show phone screen with analytics" | "Hold up fingers and say the numbers verbally" |
| "Walk from room to room" | "Cut between shots in one static location" |
| "Hold up phone showing the app" | "Tap phone casually, describe what you see" |
| "Show laptop with dashboard" | "Lean back from desk, react to what you saw" |
| "Display specific text/numbers on screen" | "Say them out loud with hand gestures" |
| "Two phones side by side" | "Hold one phone, describe both things verbally" |
| "Before/after split" | "Two separate shots in same location, different energy" |

## Native Score Rubric (1–10)

**High = native/casual. Low = reads like an ad.**

### Green Flags (push score UP)
- **"pov:" format** — instantly native, first-person experiential
- **"me when..." / "me looking at..."** — meme format, very gen-z
- **Self-deprecating humor** — "I'm not okay", "I'm sick", "I'm confused"
- **Storytime energy** — "ok so hear me out", "so there's this girl"
- **Spite/chaos energy** — "out of pure spite", "chose violence"
- **Gatekeeping/spilling format** — "I've been gatekeeping this"
- **Confusion/disbelief** — "make it make sense", "someone explain"
- **No CTA** — content stands alone, doesn't ask for anything
- **Specific details** — "at 2am", "before my coffee got cold", "on a sunday in sweats"
- **Conversational overlay** — reads like texting, not copywriting
- **Emojis used as punctuation** — 💀 🫠 😭 💅 not ✨ 🙌 📈

### Red Flags (push score DOWN)
- **"link in bio"** — instant -3 points, screams ad
- **"scroll-stopping"** — marketing jargon nobody uses casually
- **"here's why 👇"** — engagement bait format, overplayed
- **Checklist overlays** — ✓ ✗ bullet points = ad creative format
- **"Step 1, Step 2, Step 3"** — direct response copywriting
- **"What you need:"** — product pitch structure
- **Product name in the hook** — dead giveaway it's an ad
- **"start your free trial"** — pure sales
- **"ROAS", "CPC", "ad account"** — marketer jargon, not native language
- **"The game changed. Did you?"** — LinkedIn motivational energy
- **"until I found this"** — infomercial energy
- **"old way vs new way"** — comparison ad format
- **Price comparison tables** — "$500 vs $5" formatted lists
- **📈 📊 emoji** — business/growth coded, not casual
- **Addressing viewer as "your brand"** — breaks casual fourth wall

### Score Ranges with Examples

**9–10 — Peak Native**
> "started making content at 10am out of pure spite and had 4 videos ready before my coffee got cold"
> "me looking at a full month of content that took me 20 minutes while everyone else is still editing 💅"

Reads like something a real person would post with zero commercial intent. Meme format, chaotic energy, oddly specific details.

**7–8 — Very Native**
> "pov: you spent 3 hours editing one reel and the girl who pointed her phone at her face got 200k views 💀"
> "bestie I'm making content in my bathroom with zero setup and it's outperforming everything 😭"
> "this girl in my niche posts every single day and I finally figured out how she does it and I'm SICK"

Strong native energy. Uses gen-z language patterns. The product/tool is implied, never named.

**5–6 — Mixed**
> "I went from posting once a week to every single day and my reach literally 10x'd"
> "same product, same audience — I just changed the format and went from 200 to 40k views"

Decent hooks but the transformation format and growth language ("10x'd") leak ad energy.

**3–4 — Mostly Ad**
> "your competitor is posting 5 videos a day and you're still figuring out how to edit"
> "I was literally about to hire a $2k/month social media manager until I found this"

"Until I found this" is infomercial. Lecturing the viewer about their competitor is coach energy.

**1–2 — Pure Ad**
> "the only thing between you and a full content calendar is 20 minutes and RawUGC"
> "I let AI make all my ads and my ROAS went from 1.2 to 4.8 in 6 weeks"

Product name in hook, jargon (ROAS), free trial CTA. Would get scrolled past instantly.

## CTA Rules

### Ratio
- **1/3 of ideas** get a CTA (e.g. 7 out of 21)
- **2/3 of ideas** have NO CTA — empty string, content stands on its own
- Distribute evenly across funnel phases, don't cluster

### Banned CTA Patterns
- "link in bio" — always, no exceptions
- "try it free today" / "start your free trial"
- "see what it costs" / "see how I..."
- Any direct product pitch or landing page language

### Acceptable CTA Format
Comment-keyword style only:
- "comment HOW and I'll tell you what I switched to"
- "comment SUNDAY and I'll send you the process"
- "drop 💯 if you've noticed this on your own feed"

These feel native because they mimic organic creator engagement patterns (comment-bait for the algorithm).

## Rewrite Patterns

When improving low-scoring content, use these transformations:

### Hook Rewrites
| Ad Pattern | Native Pattern |
|------------|----------------|
| "What if your brand could..." | "ok so I haven't [done X] in 3 weeks and somehow..." |
| "No [X]. No [Y]. Just..." | "bestie I'm [doing X] with zero [Y] and..." |
| "I made X ads in Y time" | "my [person] asked why I was done already and..." |
| "[Jargon] completely changed when..." | "not me getting better results by [ironic thing] 💀" |
| "You just [feature description]" | "I literally typed '[funny specific thing]' and..." |
| "If you have X you can have Y by tonight" | "started [doing X] out of pure spite and..." |
| "No X. No Y. Just Z" | "the way I used to gatekeep this but..." |
| "Every day you're not..." | "this girl in my niche [does X] and I figured out how..." |
| "[Metric] went from X to Y" | "the [worse] I make my [X] the more [Y] they get and I'm confused" |
| "The only thing between you and X is Y" | "me looking at [result] while everyone else is still [struggling] 💅" |

### Overlay Rewrites
| Ad Pattern | Native Pattern |
|------------|----------------|
| Bullet checklist (✓ ✗) | Conversational back-and-forth ("him: / me:") |
| "Step 1... Step 2..." | Timeline with personality ("10:02am: opened the app angry") |
| Price comparison table | Self-talk format ("me: about to spend $2k / my friend: absolutely not") |
| Feature list | Reaction format ("pretty ad: nothing / ugly ad: money printer") |
| "What you need:" | Short punchline ("and it's working better than the stuff I actually tried on") |

### Tone Cheat Sheet
| Instead of... | Use... |
|---------------|--------|
| Professional, polished | Messy, raw, unfiltered |
| Motivational coach | Self-deprecating, confused |
| "The game changed" | "I'm not okay" |
| Confident pitch | Bewildered discovery |
| Teaching/lecturing | Gossiping/spilling tea |
| Encouraging | Smug, chaotic, spiteful |
| Direct address ("you need...") | First person ("I literally...") |

## Data Schema

Scores are stored on the card object alongside content fields:

```json
{
  "id": "1",
  "num": 1,
  "hook": "...",
  "overlay": "...",
  "shotList": "...",
  "cta": "",
  "qualityScore": 9,
  "complexityScore": "low",
  "nativeScore": 8
}
```

- `qualityScore`: integer 1–10
- `complexityScore`: `"low"` | `"medium"` | `"high"`
- `nativeScore`: integer 1–10 (high = native, low = ad-like)
- `cta`: empty string `""` for no CTA, comment-keyword format when present
